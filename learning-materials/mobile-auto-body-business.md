## 🎨 Materials Needed

You're only using three things for small repairs:

1. **Base Coat** - The color (match using paint code from door jam)
2. **Clear Coat** - Final finish/protection
3. **Primer/Filler** - For damaged areas (small amounts)

**Color Matching:**
- Find paint code on door jam
- Use "Paint Lines" app to mix exact color
- Mix by gram weight using small scale
- Not giant buckets - small bottles

## 🔄 The 5-Step Repair Process

### Step 1: Assess Damage
- Look for: scratches, scuffs, small gouges
- Skip: major collision, dents, structural damage
- Focus: small cosmetic damage that can be fixed quickly

### Step 2: Prep
- Primer and paint will NOT hide imperfections
- Sand area smooth
- Create soft edge for blending
- Apply plastic/body filler if needed

### Step 3: Base Coat (Color)
- Don't paint entire panel
- Blend into surrounding paint
- Keep job tight and efficient

### Step 4: Clear Coat
- Apply over repair area
- Let cure
- Recreates factory finish

### Step 5: Finish Work
- Wet sand if needed
- Buff to match rest of panel
- Make repair invisible

**Pro Tip:** Same workflow every time with minor variations. Practice makes perfect.

## 📍 Getting Customers

### Google Business Profile (MOST IMPORTANT)
Setup requirements:
- Business name + service area (city + suburbs)
- Categories: Auto Painting, Mobile Auto Painting
- Description: "bumper scuffs, scratches, small paint repairs, mobile"
- Before/after photos (lots of them!)
- Get 5 reviews fast

### Why Google Works
- People search "scratch repair near me" with money ready
- You show up when they're already looking
- Reviews build trust with strangers

### Marketing Photos
**Rule:** Take before/after from same angle, same distance, good daylight

- Show damage clearly
- Show finished result
- Post to Google & social media

### Additional Sources
- Instagram Reels
- Facebook local groups (community pages, neighborhood groups)
- Keep it simple: clear photo + "mobile bumper scuff repair in [city]"

**Don't Chase:**
- Dealerships (low margins, high competition)
- Residential market is wide open

## 💵 Pricing Strategy

### Three Buckets

| Bucket | Type | Price Range |
|--------|------|-------------|
| 1 - Small | Bumper scuffs, small scratches | Lower end |
| 2 - Medium | Bigger scuffed areas, more prep | Middle |
| 3 - Large | Deep gouges, body filler work | Higher end |

**Target:** $600-$800 for most single repairs

- Customer compares to body shop hassle (not detailing)
- Not bargain basement, not full repaint
- Value: convenience + speed + professional result

### Minimum Price
- Even tiny jobs cost drive time, setup, tear down
- Set minimum to ensure every job is worth it
- Keeps you from filling day with low-profit work

## 🤝 Sales Process

### Information to Get
Customer sends:
- 2-3 photos (1 close-up, 1 from few steps back)
- Year, make, model, zip code

### The Flow
1. **Get Info Fast** - Filters time wasters
2. **Diagnose & Recommend** - Position as professional: "Yep, good candidate. Here's what I'd do..."
3. **Show Proof** - "I did one almost identical last week. Here's before/after"
4. **Give Price Cleanly** - "For that repair, you're looking at [price]" Then STOP TALKING
5. **Close with Options** - "I can get you in [day/time A] or [day/time B]. Which works?"

### Objection Handlers

**"That's more than I expected"**
> "Totally fair. Just so you know, we're 40-60% less than a body shop. You're paying for convenience and same-day service without the hassle."

**"Let me think about it"**
> "No problem. I'll hold a time slot for today. Tomorrow or Friday if you decide to move forward?"

**"I got a lower quote"**
> "Some people quote low to get the business. Are they coming to your home and performing the repair same day? If lowest price is the goal, they might be a better fit. If convenience and clean result is the goal, that's exactly what I do."

### Key Sales Tips
- Don't overexplain
- Don't hesitate on price
- Don't ramble
- Clear and direct wins
- Right customers value convenience and will pay for it

## ❌ Profit Killers (Mistakes to Avoid)

### 1. Taking Wrong Jobs
- Don't do multiple panels, heavy body work
- Stay small, repeatable cosmetic repairs
- That's where speed and margins come from

### 2. Overcomplicating Process
- Don't need a shop
- Don't need perfect conditions
- Same workflow every time

### 3. Rushing Prep or Finish
- If you rush, it shows immediately
- Edges, lines, mismatch, texture will be visible
- If only one job for the day, take your time

### 4. Charging Too Low
- Don't discount too fast
- Don't compete on price
- You're selling specialized result + mobile convenience
- Right customers will pay for that

### 5. No System
- Need system for: getting customers, pricing consistently, running workflow
- Without system = hobby, not business
