## Why Sex Matters

Research shows people who have sex once a week live **49% longer** than those who only have sex once a year. For every 100 orgasms men have, they live **13% longer**. Sex isn't just pleasure—it's a window into your overall health.

## The 4 Pillars of Sexual Health

### 1. Fuel (Diet)

**Best diet for sexual function: Mediterranean diet**
- Healthy fats (avocados, nuts, olive oil)
- Leafy greens, colorful fruits
- **Pistachios**: 100g daily showed decrease in erectile dysfunction
- **Blueberries**: ~20% improvement in erectile function
- Fiber converts to short-chain fatty acids that protect blood vessels
- **Lycopene** (red fruits) helps with erections

Being overweight increases aromatase, which converts testosterone to estrogen—leading to lower testosterone and more body fat (especially around the genitals).

### 2. Strength (Exercise)

**Cardiovascular exercise:**
- **150 minutes/week** of moderate exercise = same improvement as Viagra
- Even 20 minutes of walking with a 5-minute warm-up and cool-down improved erectile function by 70% in men with heart disease
- Any cardiovascular exercise improves blood flow to penis and clitoris

**Resistance training:**
- Heavy lifting (legs, glutes, back) increases testosterone
- Muscle mass decreases ~7% per decade after 40
- Men who maintain muscle mass through resistance training are **3x less likely** to have erectile dysfunction

**Pelvic floor muscles:**
- Surround the penis and anus (in men) and vagina and anus (in women)
- Contract during orgasm (8-second rhythmic contractions)
- Help with ejaculate propulsion
- **Tight pelvic floors** can cause:
  - Erectile dysfunction
  - Premature ejaculation
  - Difficulty urinating
  - Pain during sex
- **Weak pelvic floors** can cause:
  - Less sensation
  - Difficulty reaching orgasm
- Kegels help if your pelvic floor is normal, but if it's tight, you need **relaxation exercises** (diaphragmatic breathing, happy baby pose, figure-four stretch)

### 3. Environment

**Sleep:**
- Men sleeping 5 hours vs 8 hours: **15% drop in testosterone** after just one week
- Sleep apnea reduces testosterone significantly—treating it can increase testosterone by 200 ng/dL
- Measure your neck: >17 inches (men) or >16 inches (women) suggests possible sleep apnea

**Stress:**
- Chronic stress raises cortisol, which blocks testosterone
- Raises sympathetic nervous system, preventing arousal
- 6-second kiss, 20-second hug can help break stress loops

**Endocrine-disrupting chemicals (EDCs):**
- Plastics (BPA, phthalates) mimic estrogen and reduce testosterone
- Don't heat plastic, don't leave food in plastic in the fridge
- Use glass or metal containers
- Ceramic non-stick pans better than Teflon

### 4. Confidence

- **Knowledge**: Understand anatomy (yours and your partner's)
- **Communication**: Talk openly about what feels good
- **Curiosity**: Explore different types of stimulation
- **Novelty**: Sex can become routine like eating the same ice cream—add small challenges (different position, location, pillow) to enter "flow state"

## How Arousal Works

**Men:**
- Erection = psychological story + physical stimulation
- Need to be in parasympathetic ("rest and digest") state
- Sympathetic state (stress, anxiety) prevents erections

**Women:**
- Clitoris has internal structure that extends deep into pelvis
- G-spot = area where internal clitoris and Skene's glands (prostate homologue) are located
- 85% of women need clitoral stimulation to orgasm
- Only 40% of women squirt (fluid comes from bladder + Skene's glands)

**Morning erections:**
- Men get 3-5 erections per night, up to 40 minutes each
- Caused by testosterone spikes during sleep
- Women have nocturnal clitoral tumescence too

**Orgasms:**
- Men: 8-second rhythmic pelvic floor contractions
- Women: more variable
- Post-orgasm clarity (feeling clear-headed) is common
- Some people experience post-coital dysphoria (sadness after orgasm)—not fully understood

## Common Issues

### Erectile Dysfunction (ED)

- Defined: inability to maintain erection sufficient for intercourse
- **52% of men over 50** have ED; increases 10% per decade
- Organic ED can predict heart disease 3-5 years in advance ("canary in the coal mine")
- 14% of men with ED will have a heart attack within 7 years

**Causes:**
- Poor blood vessel health
- Diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol
- Low testosterone
- Psychological (performance anxiety)
- Porn addiction/habituation to specific stimulation

**Porn-induced ED:**
- If you can get erect with porn but not with a partner, it's often psychological
- Different arousal patterns, "death grip" (masturbation technique that can't be replicated by a partner)
- Morning erections tell you if the hardware works—if you have those, it's likely psychological

### Performance Anxiety

- Thinking about "performing" instead of enjoying
- Creates sympathetic nervous system activation
- **Cycle**: anxious → can't get erection → anxious about that → can't get erection
- **Solution**: Take penetration off the table. Sensate focus—explore each other's bodies without goal-oriented sex. Once anxiety fades, erections return naturally.

### Premature Ejaculation

- Common
- Can be related to tight pelvic floor muscles
- Talk to a doctor about topical treatments or techniques

### Low Testosterone

**Symptoms:**
- Fatigue
- Brain fog
- Depression
- Low desire
- Erectile dysfunction
- Increased fat mass, decreased muscle

**Normal range:** 214-600 ng/dL (varies by individual)

**Low testosterone doubles mortality risk.** Very high testosterone (>1800) increases risk of blood clots, heart issues.

**TRT (Testosterone Replacement Therapy):**
- Only for truly low levels + symptoms
- **70% of men on TRT become infertile** after 18 months
- Can take 2 years for fertility to return after stopping
- Alternatives for men wanting fertility: hCG, clomid, enclomiphene

## Anatomy

**Penis:**
- Corpora cavernosa = spongy tissue that fills with blood
- Without regular erections (nighttime or otherwise), tissue can become fibrotic (scarred), causing shrinkage
- Average size: 5.3-5.5 inches erect

**Clitoris:**
- Internally extends back around the vagina
- Same tissue as penis (homologous)
- Most sensitive part is the glans (external)
- Can also be stimulated internally through the vagina

**Pelvic floor:**
- Bowl of muscles at base of pelvis
- Support bladder, rectum, uterus
- Attach to hip bones, sacrum, pubic symphysis
- Can get tight from hip/back injuries, stress, or poor posture

## Sex Positions & Orgasms

- **Woman on top** = more control, higher orgasm rates
- **Coital alignment technique (CAT)** = rocking motion where pubic bone rubs against clitoris
- Not every position works for every couple—experiment
- Real sex ≠ porn—communicate with your partner

## Squirting

- ~40% of women squirt
- Fluid comes from bladder + Skene's glands
- Not urine, but contains some urine
- PSA (prostate-specific antigen) present
- Theories:
  1. Antibacterial flush to prevent UTIs
  2. Hormonal trigger for ovulation (vestigial)
  3. Mate selection reward system

---

## UTIs After Sex

- Not from partner's bacteria
- Caused by friction moving bacteria from outside to urethra
- Women have short urethras—more susceptible
- Theory: squirting may flush bacteria out (evolutionary advantage)

## Pornography

- Not inherently bad
- Fantasy, not reality
- Can be problematic if:
  - Used to escape negative feelings
  - Causes guilt/shame
  - Creates unrealistic expectations
  - Leads to compulsive use
- Couples who watch porn together tend to have higher relationship satisfaction
- Desensitization possible but usually reversible

## Communicating with Your Partner

1. **Don't** bring up issues right before/after sex
2. **Pick a neutral time**—while walking, driving, not face-to-face
3. **Start positive**: "I really enjoy our time together..."
4. **Be curious**, not accusatory
5. **Ask**: "What's going on with you?"
6. **Share your truth**: "I miss what we used to have"
7. **Listen without getting defensive**

## GLP-1s (Ozempic, etc.) and Libido

- Weight loss = better blood flow = potentially better function
- "Ozempic penis" = more visible penis as fat pad shrinks
- **Potential concern**: GLP-1s work on reward pathways; may decrease desire for other rewards including sex
- Monitor: if desire drops after starting GLP-1, talk to your doctor about dose

## Fertility & Testosterone

- Low testosterone often correlates with poor semen parameters
- Semen quality is a biomarker of overall health
- Men with poor sperm counts have higher mortality rates
- Testosterone replacement **decreases fertility** significantly
- If you want kids later, discuss alternatives with your doctor (hCG, clomid)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Sexual health = overall health** (erections = blood flow)
2. **Food, exercise, sleep, stress** are the foundation
3. **Communicate** with your partner
4. **Relax** to get aroused—stress blocks it
5. **Nighttime erections** indicate healthy function
6. **ED can be a warning sign** of heart disease
7. **Pelvic floor matters**—too tight or too weak causes problems
8. **Porn ≠ real sex**
9. **More sex = longer life** (statistically)
10. **If something's wrong, see a doctor**—there's usually something you can do

## When to See a Doctor

- Persistent erectile dysfunction
- No morning erections
- Pain during sex
- Significant change in libido
- Concerns about fertility
- Anything that worries you
