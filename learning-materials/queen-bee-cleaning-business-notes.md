## Chapter 1: Intro & Background

### Chris's Story
- Started as an immigrant working different jobs
- Wife came up with cleaning business idea in 2015
- Initially selling cars, wife convinced him to try cleaning
- 3 months in, wife was making more money than his car sales job
- Quit his job and went full-time on cleaning business

### Key Quote
> "I think it's the best time to start a cleaning business. Not everyone is doing that."

### Pandemic Effect
- Lost more than half of customers March-June 2020
- After that: surge in demand - everyone wanted homes clean/disinfected
- Monthly revenue doubled ($60K → $120K)

## Chapter 8: Airbnb Cleaning

### The Model
- Not just cleaning - full **co-hosting service**
- Hosts live in China, Europe, East Coast
- Queen Bee handles everything:
  - Welcome guests
  - Laundry
  - Supplies
  - Problems between stays

### Pricing
- **$400-500 per turnover** (includes everything: laundry, supplies, cleaning)
- 3 people per unit
- 3-4 hours per home
- **Net profit: ~$80/job** (20%)
- Do ~18 jobs of this size per month

### Margins on Airbnb vs Regular Cleaning
- Cleaning only = very small margins
- **Making most money = charging % of management fee**
- Charge **10-20% of gross revenue** the unit generates
- Use software **AIRDNA** to check nightly rates in area

### Airbnb Agreement
- Minimum 3-month contract
- If they don't like service, can stop after 3 months
- Belief: they'll see results and stay

## Chapter 10: Services Offered

### Three Types

1. **Home Service** (homeowners)
   - Recurring revenue - bread & butter
   - People who pay for cleaning every 2-4 weeks
   - Less time = more money as you optimize

2. **Move Out/Move In**
   - Profitable but sometimes messy (trash)
   - Takes longer than it should

3. **Airbnb Cleanings**
   - Really good business when done right

### Recurring Revenue is Key
> "That's where the money is... you start doing those jobs in a lesser time. So you start optimizing the time."

## Chapter 11: Advice for Getting Started

### Two Key Pieces of Advice

1. **Invest in Paid Ads**

   - Works, but paid ads = next level

2. **Know Your Numbers**

   - Understanding financials saves you from big fines

## Chapter 12: Seasonal Strategy

### Slow Season
- December, January, February (winter)
- How he handles it: **Text marketing**
  - Uses instead of email marketing
  - 90% open rate!
  - Send crazy deals: "$50 off today"
  - Send to 1500 people → 10% reply "yes I want the service"
  - Keep employees busy during slow times
  - Take discount hit but keep team engaged

### Key Quote
> "What I'm trying to do during those times is just keep my employees busy. So they make money. And you know, I take a little bit of a hit on discount, my prices, but at the same time I'm taking care of my employees."

## Chapter 15: How to Price

### His Method
1. Call companies in your area
2. Ask: "How much for a 3 bedroom, 2 bathroom?"
3. Get multiple quotes
4. Adjust based on:
   - Your reputation level
   - Market rates
   - You're probably underbidding

### First Job Story
- Charged $90 for move-out
- Wife spent 12 hours → big loss
- Learned: check what market is paying!

## Chapter 16: Reviews

### How to Get Reviews
- Call customer **the next day**
- Ask if happy with service
- Ask for feedback (good or bad)
- Helps improve company

### Handling Negative Reviews
- Can't make everyone happy
- Respond professionally
- Ask if there's anything else you can do
- **Respond to EACH comment** - people see this
- Key to fast growth = good reputation
