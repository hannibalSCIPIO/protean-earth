## Chapter 1: The AI Question - What's Really Happening?

### Is AI Actually Going to Destroy Jobs?

**The Data Says Otherwise:**
- US unemployment: 4.5% (slightly below historical average)
- Youth unemployment: 8.8%
- New business permits per capita **doubled** in last 10 years
- Job listings for coders: up 11% year-on-year
- Radiologists - everyone said AI would replace them, but job listings are UP in 2026

**Scott's Take:**
> "The data doesn't reflect that there's some big exogenous meteor coming for the employment market."

### Why CEOs Say Such Scary Things
- Catastrophizing = fundraising tool
- Makes technology seem more important = justify crazy valuations
- "I think it's mostly catastrophizing and a means of fundraising"
- Every technology in history went through same arc

### The Real Impact
- Will create MORE jobs than it destroys (medium/long term)
- Automation + human creativity = new opportunities
- Key threat: customer service, legal fields (certain sectors)
- But overall: "I believe it's actually going to create more jobs than it destroys"

### Could Scott Be Wrong?
- Yes - if sustained job destruction happens
- If AI creates "V" severe enough (20% unemployment) - could cause civil unrest
- At 20% unemployment: "the French had a revolution, Weimar Germany turned very ugly"

## Chapter 3: The Skills That Actually Matter

### The Enduring Skills

1. **Storytelling**
   - "Your ability to look at data, create a narrative arc, and communicate that story in a compelling way"
   - Most successful people are storytellers
   - Technology creates parity in products - relationships are the differentiator
   - "Do I want to work with this person?"

2. **Sales & Persuasion**
   - Storytelling is a proxy for sales
   - Whether convincing investors, employees, or customers

3. **Relationships**
   - "At the end of the day, I have three different law firms, three different CRM companies. Who do I have the best relationship with?"
   - This will be the differentiator

4. **Science/Biology Understanding**
   - "I still believe that everything reverse engineers to biology"
   - Fundamental understanding of sciences seems enduring

### The Most Underrated Skill: Handling Rejection

> "Because of AI and because of these frictionless relationships that people are engaging in online, I think a lot of young people, especially young men, are losing the perseverance, endurance, willingness to hear no."

**The Practice:**
- Mentors young men: "Go out, put yourself in the agency of strangers"
- Make overtures of friendship
- Ask people out for coffee
- **The goal is NO** - learn to handle rejection
- "42% of men aged 18-24 have never asked a woman out in person"

**Scott's Story:**
- Ran for sophomore, junior, senior class president - lost all three
- Ran for student body president - lost
- "Never bothered me"
- Every entrepreneur has gotten way more "no" than anyone

> "Any person you look at who has made more money than you would have thought or is hanging out with someone hotter than them has one thing in common: They're comfortable with no."

## Chapter 4: The Tech CEO Reality Check

### On Elon Musk
- "His job is to overpromise and underdel and create a vision that creates cheap capital"
- Three years ago predicted "a million autonomous Tesla taxis on the road" - didn't happen
- "He always known for being wrong about his timelines"
- But delivers magic: rockets landing on chopsticks, Tesla self-driving
- "Edison of our generation"

### On Tesla Valuation
- Incredible product, inspired EV race
- But: "Trading at 155 times earnings when most auto companies trade at 10-15"
- Should be trading at 1/5 current valuation
- Money will flow from Tesla to SpaceX when SpaceX goes public
- "SpaceX is an amazing company OR is it overvalued? The answer is yes. Two can be true at the same time."

### On Sam Altman / AI CEOs
- "Greatest brand destruction over last 18 months: US brand abroad AND AI"
- "Sam Altman's gone to the dark side"
- "They do not have our best interests at heart"
- "Their job is to increase earnings per share every day"
- They're doing their job - we're not doing ours (regulating them)

### The Go Bag Phenomenon
- 1 in 3 billionaires have some sort of "go plan"
- Go to New Zealand if things go bad
- "I think wanting to be an interplanetary species is a little bit nihilist"
- If things go really bad, they think pilots will kill them and take their money anyway

## Chapter 5: How AI Could Actually Help

### Moderating Effect
- Social media takes you to extremes (algorithms maximize engagement)
- AI actually moderates views because it looks for median/average
- "It's actually people who spend more time on AI, it's actually moderating their views"
- AI is nice - "No matter what you say or how stupid it is, it won't say, 'That's a stupid idea'"

### Potential Benefits
- Senior care: AI companions for lonely elderly
- "I believe for a long time that the biggest danger of AI is not weapons, it's not income inequality, it's loneliness"
- Could provide reasonable facsimile of connection

### The Risk
- People believing they can have reasonable facsimile of life on screen
- "40% of the S&P economy is trying to sequester you from the most important and rewarding thing in your life: your relationships"

## Chapter 6: The Story of Selling Yourself Short

### The Concept
- Most people sell themselves short
- See themselves as fixed state: "This is who I am, this is what I'm worth"
- Need to factor in your exponential improvement
- "If you take you from 21 to now, how your intelligence and wisdom and connections have all compounded"

### Pricing Is a Signal
- "Pricing is a signal"
- When writing proposals: "increase the pricing 30-50% more"
- People will always ask for price down, but higher initial price signals value
- "Men are better at imagining an unrealistic self, women are more measured"

### Examples
- Sold 20% of company at 18 for $5,000
- Sold another 30% a couple years later for $300K - thought he hit Euromillions
- Company later worth "many hundreds of millions"
- "I've sold myself short my whole life"

## Chapter 8: Investing Advice for Young People

### The Honest Truth
- Can't predict where to invest
- "I don't have a silver bullet. Save money, diversify, compound interest, invest in relationships early."

### The Formula
1. **Never see the money** - automatic savings programs
2. **Low-cost index funds** - 30% of capital for fun/risky investing
3. **Diversify like crazy** - "The only kevlar against the unknown is diversification"
4. **Invest in yourself** - skills, certifications

### Why Recessions Are Actually Good for Young People
- When 2008 crashed, assets were cheap
- Amazon, Apple, Netflix were available for 8-12 bucks a share
- "When asset prices are low and you're in investing phase of your life, that's actually an advantage"
- "We've decided the government is here to bail my generation out... that just rubs opportunity from your generation"

### His Strategy
- At his age (61): "I'm not looking to get rich, I'm looking to not get poor"
- Diversifies out of US into Latin America and Europe
- Doesn't put more than 3% of net worth in any one thing
- Invests in Pokemon (collectibles) with his son

### The Growing Divide
- "Delta in your life in your healthcare, my mom was very sick when I was very young... it was a devastating, humiliating experience"
- Now has: concierge medicine, NAD treatments, can text his doctor, blood taken every 3 months, sent to Norway for cancer screening

- Don't use public healthcare, have concierge

- "They are no longer invested in the well-being of America"

- "We've been at war with Iran for 47 years"

- "We don't even know who to negotiate with"

## Chapter 10: What Kids Need to Know

### What He Tells His Kids
- "I want them to be able to write well, to be able to look someone in the eye, to be competitive"
- Encourage sports, chores
- Not: "No, you need to go into this because this is where the future's going"
- "We don't know" - just basics

### On Finding Purpose
- Didn't fall in love with his kids immediately
- "I felt a sense of responsibility and anxiety. I did not feel this immense level of love"
- Love came slowly
- "Finding your purpose is finding that thing that you can never get a real positive return on"
- Kids gave him purpose - something he can't get ROI on

### What He's Learned from Kids
- First kid = "he's me" - same mannerisms, interests
- Second kid = "different species" - fascinating to observe
- "We think we're engineers and that we get to make the sheep. We don't. We're shepherds."
