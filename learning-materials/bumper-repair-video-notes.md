## Step 1: Clean the Area
- Use **wax and grease remover**
- Clean damaged area AND surrounding areas
- Why: Removes wax/grease/grime so you don't grind it into plastic when sanding
- Important for tape/paper to stick properly

---

## Step 2: Tape Off Sensitive Areas
- Tape off plastic trim, sensors, etc.
- Damage was close to plastic trim - taped it off to protect
- **Sensors:** Tape them off - up to 8mm of material won't affect sensor, but tape prevents any buildup
- Don't want to accidentally damage trim while sanding

---

## Step 3: Sand the Damage
- Use **180 grit** sandpaper
- DA (dual action) sander
- Sand off damage that's sticking up
- Removes gouges from the impact

---

## Step 4: Clean Again
- Blow off dust
- Wipe down with wax and grease remover again

---

## Step 5: Apply Filler (Skim Coat)
- For minor gouges in plastic
- Mix filler: 2% hardener to filler
- **Rule of thumb:** If you have a golf ball of filler, use a "pee" of hardener
- Only mix what you need (~3 minutes working time in heat)
- Apply very light skim coat
- Don't remove too much material if you can avoid it

### How to Tell When Filler is Ready
- Take fingernail and press in
- If it doesn't leave a mark → ready to sand

---

## Step 6: Sand Filler
- Use **320 grit**
- Always wear a mask when sanding filler
- Hand sand to refine - feels with hand (glove on)
- Check for any gouges you missed
- Sometimes gouges become visible after sanding (panel goes "milky")

---

## Step 7: Fill Any Missed Spots
- Mix a little more filler if needed
- Sand again with 320

---

## Step 8: Scuff for Primer
- Use **red 3M scuff pad**
- Scuff entire area where you'll apply primer

---

## Step 9: Apply High Build Primer
- **Why aerosol/rattle can primer?**
  - Faster (no cleaning gun - 5-10 min saved)
  - Same quality as spray gun primer
  - Mobile environment = speed + efficiency
- Primer is **self-etching** - no need for 1K primer before
- Applies 2-3 coats
- Self-etching = keeps out moisture/water
- Use heat gun to flash it quickly (mobile = speed)

---

## Step 10: Sand Primer
- Same scuff pad to knock off primer texture
- Check on the "flop" (light reflection)
- Feel with hand - should be "buttery smooth"

---

## Step 11: Prepare for Paint
- Tape off sensors
- Lightly scuff where you'll apply base coat and clear coat
- Blow off dust
- Clean with wax and grease remover

---

## Step 12: Find Color Code
- Usually inside door jamb
- On Hondas/Acuras: right there in door jamb
- This car: **NH893P** (P = pearl)

---

## Step 13: Mix Paint
- Mixed 2 ounces (probably only need 1-1.5 oz)
- Uses **waterborne paint**
  - Wet = milky consistency (impossible to mix by eye)
  - Better for environment
  - Lays down really well
- Add water activator: 5-10%

---

## Step 14: Apply Base Coat
- **Paint in the sun** - lets you see coverage better (like a giant UV light)
- Tack off any dust nibs first
- Get coverage over primer

---

## Step 15: Clear Coat
- **Clear in the shade** - easier to see it "glass up"
- Clear ratio: **2:1** (2 parts clear, 1 part activator)
- Use medium activator (~60°F day)
- Nice even strokes, 50% overlap
- Don't use bent wrist
- Find your own pace/volume style

---

## Key Principles

### Blend Lines
- Don't do blends on horizontal surfaces - blend line will fail over time
- Keep blends on **vertical surfaces**
- Go several inches past where you're applying base coat
- Use straight blender at end to melt old clear with new
- Goal: no visible blend line

### Working Plastic Bumpers
- Plastic is flexible - can see it moving as you sand
- Apply as little pressure as possible
- Let sandpaper do the work
- Don't create pressure points

### Primer Tips
- Self-etching primer = moisture barrier
- No need for separate 1K primer

### Paint Tips
- Waterborne paint flashes to true color when it dries
- Use heat gun in mobile environment to speed drying

## Pro Tips from the Video

1. **Always protect the customer's property** - driveway, etc.
2. **Clean multiple times** - before sanding, after sanding, before paint
3. **Feel with your hands** - catch what you can't see
4. **Don't over-remove material** - creates mapping issues
5. **Tape off sensors** - don't get material on them
6. **Paint in sun, clear in shade** - see coverage vs. see glass up
7. **Speed matters in mobile** - use aerosol primer, heat gun
8. **Don't cheap out on quality** - but don't overspend on fancy gear
9. **Respond quickly** - responsiveness wins jobs
10. **Underpromise, overdeliver** - $600 job, customer amazed
