# Sexual Health Basics for Teens

*A guide to understanding your body, relationships, and healthy intimacy*

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

Good sex isn't just about the act—it's about health, connection, and feeling good. Research shows people who have healthy sex lives often live longer and feel more connected to their partners.

---

## 🏠 The 4 Pillars of Sexual Health

### 1. Fuel (What You Eat)
- **Mediterranean diet** = best for sexual health (fruits, veggies, nuts, healthy fats)
- Foods like **pistachios** and **blueberries** may help blood flow
- Fiber is important—keeps blood vessels healthy
- Being overweight can affect sexual function

### 2. Strength (Exercise)
- **150 min/week** of cardio = same improvement as Viagra for erection quality
- Any exercise that gets your heart pumping helps blood flow to genitals
- **Pelvic floor muscles** matter—they help with erections and orgasms
- Resistance training (lifting weights) helps maintain testosterone

### 3. Environment (Stress & Sleep)
- **Sleep**: 5 hours vs 8 hours = 15% drop in testosterone
- **Stress**: Chronic stress raises cortisol, which lowers testosterone
- Sleep apnea can hurt testosterone levels
- Limit plastics—some chemicals can affect hormones

### 4. Confidence (Communication)
- Talk openly with partners about what you like
- Don't expect your partner to read your mind
- Being curious about what feels good for both of you matters

---

## 🧠 How Arousal Works

- Erections = a "story" in your brain + physical stimulation
- You need to be relaxed (parasympathetic nervous system) to get an erection
- Performance anxiety can actually prevent erections
- Focus on enjoying the moment, not "performing"

---

## 🩺 What's Normal?

- **Morning erections** = sign of healthy blood flow
- Erectile dysfunction (ED) is common—affects 52% of men over 50, but also younger guys
- ED can be a warning sign of heart disease (the penis is a "canary in the coal mine")
- If you can't get an erection sometimes, that doesn't automatically mean ED

---

## 📱 Tech & Dopamine

- Too much screen time/social media can affect your ability to focus and be present
- Porn is fantasy—it doesn't show real sex
- Some people get so used to certain porn that real intimacy feels different

---

## 💬 Talking to Your Partner

- Don't bring up sex issues right before or after sex
- Pick a relaxed time, maybe on a walk
- Start with something positive: "I really enjoy our time together..."
- Ask questions, be curious about how your partner feels

---

## ⚠️ Common Concerns

**"Is my penis normal?"**
- Average penis size: ~5.3-5.5 inches erect
- Most women don't need a huge penis for pleasure—clitoral stimulation is key
- Size anxiety is common but usually unnecessary

**"What if I can't perform?"**
- It happens to almost everyone at some point
- Don't panic—talk to your partner, relax, try again later
- If it keeps happening, talk to a doctor

**"Is pornography bad?"**
- Not necessarily, but it sets unrealistic expectations
- It can affect arousal if you're used to very specific stimulation
- Talk to a partner if it causes issues in your relationship

---

## 🌟 The Big Takeaways

1. **Your body is connected**—heart health = sexual health
2. **Sleep, food, and exercise matter** more than you think
3. **Communication is key**—talk to your partner
4. **Stress kills intimacy**—make time to be present
5. **It's okay to not be ready**—consent is everything
6. **If something feels wrong, see a doctor**—don't suffer in silence
