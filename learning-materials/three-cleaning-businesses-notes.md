A local business that can be managed completely remotely

## Chapter 8: Customer Service = Competitive Advantage

### The Phone Answering Strategy
- **Answer 7am-11pm, 7 days a week** (Queen Bee)
- Most competitors don't pick up
- "Picking up the phone is a competitive advantage"

### Close Rate Data
- **Pick up in first couple rings: 50-60% close rate**
- **Call back in a few minutes: 16.5% close rate**
- Huge difference!

### Global Talent
- Use team in South Africa/Philippines
- RingCentral for routing calls
- Never miss a call

### Automated Follow-up
- AI texts missed callers

## Chapter 10: Pricing Strategy

### How to Price
1. Call competitors in your area
2. Ask: "How much for 3BR/2BA?"
3. Get multiple quotes
4. Adjust for your reputation level
5. You're probably underbidding

### Chris's Story
- First job: charged $90, wife worked 12 hours
- Learned: check market rates!

### Airbnb Pricing

## Chapter 14: Reviews & Reputation

### Getting Reviews
- Call **next day** after service
- Ask: "Any problems? Can you leave feedback?"
- Automated system: text → happy/sad face → one-click review

### The Review Trick
- Ask cleaner to request review
- Customers more likely to leave review for person they met
- Offer cleaner $25 bonus per review
- Company auto-texts customer easy review link
- Win-win

### Handling Negative Reviews
- Respond professionally to EVERY comment
- "People see that"
- Ask: "What else can I do for you?"
- Sometimes you can't make everyone happy
- 7% will reverse after you resolve issue

## Chapter 16: AirBnB Cleaning Business

### The Model (Chris)
- Not just cleaning - **co-hosting**
- Hosts live China, Europe, East Coast
- Take care of everything:
  - Welcome guests
  - Laundry
  - Supplies
  - Problems between stays

### Pricing Structure
- Cleaning only = small margins
- **Charge 10-20% of gross revenue**
- Use **AIRDNA** software to check nightly rates
- Agreement: 3-month minimum

### Operational Costs (Chris)
- 2-3 cleaners per unit
- 3-4 hours per home
- Labor: ~$50 (2 people × 2 hours × $25)
- Supplies: ~$30
- Parking: ~$20-30
- **Total cost: ~$100 → Charge $350+ = great ROI**

### The Airbnb Tips

**Tip 1: Start Small**
- Can't charge high rates initially
- Get experience, get reviews
- Then raise rates

**Tip 2: Don't Do Laundry On Site**
- Hosts ask: "Do cleaning and laundry on site"
- **99% of time = NO**
- Finish cleaning, wait for laundry = kills profits
- Use laundromat: $2.97/pound
- Charge host $3.79/pound
- Make $1/pound without touching inventory

**Tip 3: Act Like Property Manager**
- Owners traveling (one in Bhutan currently)
- Take pictures, document problems
- Send to owner
- Be the point of contact
- That's how you make money

## Chapter 17: The Sales Script (Neel's Roleplay)

### The Psychological Close
1. **Build rapport** - "Welcome to Los Angeles"
2. **Clarify** - "Tell me what you're looking for"
3. **Take control** - Ask questions to price properly
4. **Never offer one-time option** - Ask "monthly, weekly, or biweekly?"
5. **Anchor high** - Throw out higher price first
6. **Handle objections** - Value proposition, peace of mind, guarantees
7. **Guarantee** - "Free 2-hour reclean if anything missed"

### Key Psychological Trick
- Don't ask: "Do you want one-time or recurring?"
- Ask: "Weekly, biweekly, or monthly?"
- They answer with what you want

## Chapter 21: Scaling Advice

### The Roadmap to $1 Million

1. **Website** - Don't spend $20K, start simple, upgrade quarterly
2. **Ad Spend** - Figure out 1-2 channels, don't waste money
3. **Start small** - Can't handle traffic you can't deliver
4. **Scale gradually** - Hire 4-5 cleaners/week (later stage)
5. **Process first** - "New process + growth = disaster"
6. **Get reviews** - Reputation = trust

### Marketing Spend by Stage
- **Early stage:** 15-20% of revenue on marketing
- **Mature:** 5% of revenue
- "Overspend first year to scale, then level down"

### Why Businesses Fail
- Lack of customers
- Don't know how to bring more in
- Customers cancel, no new ones coming
